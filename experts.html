View of the Experts

Using an extensive state level data set ranging from 1977 to 1997, Professor H. Naci Mocan and R. Kaj Gittings of the University of Colorado, Denver found that for every five commutations of a death sentence, five additional murders resulted

Dr. David Muhlhausen (earlier cited in this paper) continues on the importance of the deterrent factor by stating, “How capital punishment affects murder rates can be explained through general deterrence theory, which supposes that increasing the risk of apprehension and punishment for crime deters individuals from committing crime”(Muhlhausen, 2007). The Deterrence theory assumes criminals to be responsive to cost/ benefit analysis when deciding whether to commit a crime or not.
